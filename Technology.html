Technology Books

The Cathedral and the Bazaar

Eric S. Raymond

One-line summary: Explores two contrasting models of software development — the closed cathedral and the open bazaar — and argues why open-source wins

Introduction to Information Retrieval

Manning, Raghavan & Schütze

One-line summary: A Stanford textbook that explains how search engines work — from indexing and ranking to text classification and clustering

Think Python

Allen B. Downey

One-line summary: A beginner-friendly introduction to Python programming, designed to teach you how to think like a computer scientist from the ground up

Networks, Crowds, and Markets

Easley & Kleinberg

One-line summary: A Cornell University book that connects economics, sociology, and technology to explain how the social and digital worlds are interlinked

Technology Innovation

General Topic

one-line summary :how technologies are developed and used to improve products and businesses

Life 3.0

Max Tegmark

One-line summary: Explores the future of artificial intelligence and how it could reshape life, intelligence, and humanity’s role in the universe

Hooked

Nir Eyal

One-line summary: Explains how companies create habit-forming products using a four-step “Hook Model” that drives user engagement and repeated use

The Phoenix Project

Gene Kim, Kevin Behr

One-line summary: A business novel that shows how DevOps principles can transform IT teams and improve efficiency by fixing bottlenecks, collaboration issues, and workflow processes.

AI Superpowers

Kai-Fu Lee

One-line summary: Compares the AI race between China and the U.S., explaining how artificial intelligence will reshape global power, economies, and the future of work

Information Technology

Varies

One-line summary: Covers the basics of computers , software, and networks used to manage and process information.

Chip War

Chris Miller

One-line summary: Explains the global battle over semiconductor technology and how control of computer chips shapes economic power, innovation, and geopolitics.

Quantum Robotics

Specific Will

One-line summary: Combines Quantum computing concepts with robotice to improve products and bussinesses
